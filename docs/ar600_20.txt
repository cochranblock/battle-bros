Chapter 1 - Introduction
Section 1-1 - Purpose
AR 600-20 prescribes the policy and responsibility of command, which includes military discipline, the Army Equal Opportunity Program, the Army Sexual Harassment/Assault Response and Prevention (SHARP) Program, and other matters relating to command authority.

Chapter 4 - Military Discipline and Conduct
Section 4-1 - Military Discipline
Military discipline is founded upon self-discipline, respect for properly constituted authority, and the embracing of the professional Army ethic with its supporting individual values. Military discipline will be developed by individual and group training to create a mental attitude resulting in proper conduct and prompt obedience to lawful military authority.

Section 4-2 - Standards of Conduct
Soldiers are expected to maintain the highest standards of conduct at all times. This includes both on-duty and off-duty behavior. Conduct unbecoming a member of the military service includes action or behavior in an unofficial or private capacity which, in dishonoring or disgracing the Soldier personally, seriously compromises the person's standing as a Soldier.

Section 4-3 - Relationships Between Soldiers of Different Rank
Relationships between Soldiers of different rank are prohibited if they compromise or appear to compromise the integrity of supervisory authority or chain of command, cause actual or perceived partiality or unfairness, involve or appear to involve the improper use of rank or position for personal gain, are or are perceived to be exploitative or coercive in nature, or create an actual or clearly predictable adverse impact on discipline, authority, morale, or the ability of the command to accomplish its mission.

Section 4-4 - Fraternization
The term fraternization refers to relationships between officers and enlisted personnel that violate the customary bounds of acceptable senior-subordinate relationships. Fraternization is a violation of Article 134, UCMJ. All military personnel share the responsibility for maintaining professional relationships.

Section 4-5 - Hazing
Hazing is defined as any conduct whereby a military member or members, regardless of service or rank, without proper authority causes another military member or members, regardless of service or rank, to suffer or be exposed to any activity which is cruel, abusive, humiliating, oppressive, demeaning, or harmful. Hazing is prohibited.

Chapter 6 - Equal Opportunity Program
Section 6-1 - Policy
The Army provides equal opportunity and fair treatment for military personnel and family members without regard to race, color, gender, religion, national origin, or sexual orientation. Unlawful discrimination against Soldiers is prohibited and will not be tolerated.

Section 6-2 - Commander Responsibilities
Commanders at all levels are responsible for establishing a positive equal opportunity climate within their command. This includes processing complaints promptly, protecting complainants from reprisal, and taking appropriate corrective action.

Section 6-3 - Complaint Processing
Soldiers who believe they have been discriminated against may file a complaint through the chain of command, the Equal Opportunity representative, or the Inspector General. Complaints must be processed within the timeframes established by this regulation.

Chapter 7 - Sexual Harassment/Assault Response and Prevention (SHARP) Program
Section 7-1 - Policy
Sexual harassment and sexual assault are serious offenses that undermine readiness, unit cohesion, and the Army Values. The Army has a zero-tolerance policy for sexual harassment and sexual assault.

Section 7-2 - Definitions
Sexual harassment is conduct that involves unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and deliberate or repeated offensive comments or gestures of a sexual nature when submission to such conduct is made either explicitly or implicitly a term or condition of a person's job, pay, or career.

Section 7-3 - Reporting Options
There are two types of reports for sexual assault: Restricted and Unrestricted. A Restricted Report allows a victim to confidentially disclose the assault to specified individuals and receive medical treatment and counseling without triggering an official investigation. An Unrestricted Report triggers an official investigation by the appropriate authorities.

Section 7-4 - Commander Responsibilities
Commanders must ensure all Soldiers receive annual SHARP training. Commanders must immediately report any allegations of sexual assault through their chain of command. Commanders must protect victims from retaliation and ensure they receive appropriate support services.

Section 7-5 - Reprisal Prohibited
No person may restrict a Soldier from making a lawful communication to a Member of Congress, an Inspector General, or any other authorized recipient. Reprisal against any Soldier for filing a complaint or providing testimony is prohibited.

Chapter 8 - Command Authority
Section 8-1 - Responsibility
The commanding officer is responsible for everything the unit does or fails to do. Command authority is exercised through the chain of command. Officers and NCOs must lead by example.

Section 8-2 - Chain of Command
The chain of command is the succession of commanding officers from a superior to a subordinate through which command is exercised. The chain of command is the primary channel for the transmittal of orders, instructions, and information.

Section 8-3 - Noncommissioned Officer Support Channel
The NCO support channel parallels and reinforces the chain of command. NCOs are the backbone of the Army and are responsible for the training, discipline, and welfare of Soldiers.
